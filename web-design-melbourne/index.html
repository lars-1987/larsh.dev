Questions, answered

How much does a website cost?
It depends on scope, so I quote per project after a short chat rather than from a template price list. A one-page landing page and a full multi-page site sit in very different brackets. You get a fixed quote before any work starts, with no surprises.
How long does it take?
A focused landing page is usually one to two weeks. A full business website is typically three to six weeks, depending on the number of pages, how ready the content is, and the amount of custom design and functionality.
Do you do SEO?
Yes. Every site ships with clean technical SEO: fast Core Web Vitals, schema markup, a sensible content structure and local signals. It is built in from the start, not bolted on later.
Do you use WordPress or a CMS?
Yes, if you want one. Most businesses like to update their own content, so I set the site up with a CMS: WordPress if that's what you're comfortable with, or a lighter modern CMS if you'd prefer. Either way the front end is custom-built and fast, tailored to your business rather than a generic template, and the site stays yours to own.
Do you work with businesses outside Melbourne?
Melbourne is home and I know the local market, but I work with clients anywhere. The whole process is remote-friendly and time-zone flexible.
Who owns the site and the code?
You do, completely. No lock-in and no monthly ransom to keep your own website online. You get the full codebase and everything needed to host it wherever you like.
